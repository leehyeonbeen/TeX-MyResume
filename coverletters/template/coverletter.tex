    \opening{Dear Prof. Kingston,}

    I am writing to express my interest in joining your lab as a Ph.D. student in Computer Science at Purdue University, starting in Fall 2026. My research lies at the intersection of machine learning, robotics, and dynamics, with a focus on physics-aware modeling of contact-rich dynamics.

    I am currently affiliated with Prof. Jin-Gyun Kim's group at Kyung Hee University in South Korea, where I work on learning contact force dynamics of a hydraulic manipulator. Broadly, my research has focused on data-driven, physics-aware models for complex dynamics in both simulation and the real world. I also spent time in Prof. Joseph Lim's group at KAIST, where my experience with robot learning methods and skill-prior reinforcement learning was a turning point for pursuing a Ph.D. in robotics.

    With this background, I am particularly interested in your work on reinforcement learning, constrained planning, and differentiable simulators. In my research, I have focused on incorporating physical priors into learning frameworks to facilitate robust dynamics prediction and interpretability. I believe this direction naturally connects to your interest in structured, generalizable learning methods, including constraint-aware and neurosymbolic approaches. My work on learned dynamics models would contribute to building world models that can reason about physical dynamics, and my experience with reinforcement learning for long-horizon manipulation prepares me to engage with complex manipulation problems in your lab. I am also drawn to the application of differentiable simulators for learning algorithms, and my rich background in multi-body simulation will provide a foundation for this work.

    I have submitted my application to the Computer Science Ph.D. program at Purdue. Along with this letter, I am attaching my CV and transcripts for your reference.
